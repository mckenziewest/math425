\textbf{(Supp-14)} Assume $G$ is a cyclic group with generator $g$ and $H$ is a cyclic group with generator $h$.  That is, $G=\langle g\rangle$ and $H=\langle h\rangle$.  Show that $G\times H = \langle(g,e_H),(e_G,h)\rangle$, where $e_H$ is the identity element of $H$ and $e_G$ is the identity element of $G$.
